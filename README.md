# Anteroom Reviewer Handbook

Anteroom is a structured tool for AI launch readiness. It watches a
curated regulatory corpus, surfaces verified citations with reviewer
attribution and last-verified dates, and emails subscribers a diff
when a provision changes.

This handbook describes how the corpus is reviewed, what a reviewer
does, and how to apply.

## Why reviewers exist

Most AI legal and compliance work breaks in the same place. Teams rely
on stale summaries, unattributed notes, or secondary commentary when
what they actually need is a clean path back to the source text, a
record of who reviewed it, and a fast way to see what changed.

Anteroom treats that as a verification discipline problem, not a model
quality problem. The corpus is hand-curated. Every provision carries
a named reviewer and a last-verified date. Reviewers are the reason
those fields can be trusted.

## What a reviewer does

A reviewer takes responsibility for a set of provisions inside a
specific regime (for example, "EU AI Act, Articles 6 through 15" or
"BIPA, Sections 15(a) through 15(d)"). For each provision they own,
the reviewer:

1. Confirms the current canonical text matches the primary source
2. Confirms the plain-language summary is accurate and current
3. Confirms the citation form is correct
4. Updates the last-verified date when they finish a pass
5. Flags provisions that have changed and writes the plain-language
   change summary that goes into the diff subscribers see
6. Recommends new provisions inside their regime that the corpus
   should add

Reviewers are credited by name on every provision they own, in-app
and in the public corpus.

## How often reviews happen

Each regime is on a defined cadence. Fast-moving regimes (EU AI Act
during its rollout, NIST AI RMF, sectoral AI guidance) are reviewed
monthly. Slower-moving regimes (GDPR, CCPA, ADA, established privacy
laws) are reviewed quarterly. Reviewers are also pinged when a
regulatory signal touches a provision they own, between scheduled
passes.

## What we ask of you

- Genuine working depth in the regime you would cover. Not "I read
  about it once." Actual practice or policy experience.
- Willingness to be named publicly on the provisions you verify.
- Roughly two to six hours per month, depending on regime cadence
  and corpus coverage in your area.
- Honesty about what you do not know. "Out of scope for me" is a
  valid answer.

## What you get

- Public attribution on every provision you review
- Citation on the Anteroom site as a corpus reviewer
- Access to the reviewer-only view of the corpus and the signal feed
- Visibility into how AI legal infrastructure gets built in practice

## How to apply

Send a short note to **hello@anteroom.so** with:

1. The regime or regimes you have working depth in
2. A sentence on how you have used or advised on those regimes in
   practice
3. Your LinkedIn URL or a similar pointer

Reviewer slots open on a rolling basis as corpus coverage expands.
